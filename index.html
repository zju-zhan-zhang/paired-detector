Paired Detector: Mispronunciation Detection with Native Reference

Mispronunciation detection plays an important role in Computer-Assisted Pronunciation Training (CAPT). Currently, the mainstream mispronunciation detection methods are based on the automatic speech recognition (ASR) technology. This kind of method recognizes the pronounced phonemes and aligns them to the target phonemes to find the mispronunciation. Such a process assumes that the mispronunciation can be categorized into the predefined phonemes. However, this assumption is often not valid due to the influence of the learner's mother tongue. To better discriminate the mispronunciation, we choose the robust acoustic unit as the intermediate. We convert the accented unit sequence into the native version and further use this native sequence as a reference for an implicit comparison-based detection. The experiments show that the proposed method can naturally convert the accented speech into the native version. Our method also achieves an improved F1 score on the L2-Arctic dataset for the detection task.
